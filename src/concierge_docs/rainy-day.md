# Athens Rainy Day Guide

Athens averages only 120 rainy days a year, but when it rains, here's how to spend it indoors.

## World-Class Museums (All Indoors)

### National Archaeological Museum
Greece's finest antiquities collection. Plan for 2–3 hours.
Address: 44 Patission St. Hours: Tue–Sun 09:00–20:00. Entry: €15.
Taxi from hotel: 15 min, ~€8.

### Acropolis Museum
Even on a rainy day, the hilltop views through the huge glass walls are dramatic.
Hours: Tue–Sun 09:00–20:00. Entry: €15.
Walk from hotel: 25 min through Plaka, or taxi 10 min ~€7.

### Museum of Cycladic Art
Stunning collection of 5,000-year-old marble Cycladic figurines. Compact and beautifully lit.
Address: 4 Neophytou Douka St, Kolonaki.
Hours: Mon, Wed–Fri 10:00–17:00, Thu until 20:00, Sat 10:00–17:00, Sun 11:00–17:00.
Entry: €9.

### Benaki Museum
Greek history from prehistory to the 20th century. Excellent café on the top floor.
Address: 1 Koumbari St & Vasilissis Sofias Ave. Entry: €12. Closed Tuesdays.

### Byzantine & Christian Museum
Magnificent collection of Byzantine icons and religious art in a Neoclassical villa.
Address: 22 Vasilissis Sofias Ave. Entry: €8.

## Shopping (Stay Dry, Shop Well)

### Attica Department Store (Syntagma)
Largest department store in Athens — clothing, accessories, cosmetics, gourmet food floor.
Address: City Link Mall, Panepistimiou & Voukourestiou. Open daily 10:00–21:00.

### Ermou Street
Athens' main pedestrianised shopping street runs 500m from Syntagma to Monastiraki.
H&M, Zara, Mango, and Greek brands. Partially covered arcades (stoa) offer shelter.

### Monastiraki Flea Market
Quirky shops in permanent buildings — antiques, vinyl records, leather goods, jewellery.
Most shops open even on rainy days.

### Kolonaki Boutiques
Luxury Greek and international brands in the upscale Kolonaki neighbourhood.
10-minute walk from hotel or 5-minute taxi.

## Cosy Cafés & Patisseries

- **GB Roof Garden Café** (Grande Bretagne Hotel, Syntagma): stunning views, excellent coffee.
- **Oraia Ellas** (Monastiraki): traditional sweets and loukoumades (Greek doughnuts).
- **Aristokratikon** (Voulis St): artisan chocolate and pralines since 1928.
- **Two Lizards** (Exarcheia): relaxed vintage café, excellent espresso.

## Cooking / Food Experience

### Athens Cooking Class
Several studios near the hotel offer half-day Greek cooking classes. Book via concierge.
Typical cost: €65–85/person including ingredients and meal. Duration: 3 hours.

## Spa Day at the Hotel

The hotel's Spa & Wellness Centre is ideal on a rainy day:
- Heated indoor pool, steam room, sauna.
- Treatments: massage, facials, couples packages.
- Hours: 08:00–22:00. Advance booking recommended at the spa desk.

## Evening: Theatre & Culture

- **National Theatre of Greece** (Agiou Konstantinou St): drama and opera in a stunning
  Neoclassical building. Check programme at the front desk.
- **Megaron Athens Concert Hall**: classical music, jazz, and international performances.
  Address: Vasilissis Sofias & Kokkali. Taxi ~10 min.
